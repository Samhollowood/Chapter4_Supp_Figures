# Figure Description

Each PNG file contains a four-panel summary of the atmospheric conditions associated with an individual surplus-rainfall event.


## Panel (a): HYSPLIT Back-Trajectories

Shows 120-hour HYSPLIT backward trajectories associated with the rainfall event.

Trajectories are classified according to their dominant moisture source:

* **North Atlantic** (orange)
* **Mediterranean** (pink)
* **Tropics** (green)
* **Local** (blue)
* **Residual / Unclassified** (grey)

The station location is indicated by a white triangle.

---

## Panel (b): 500 hPa Geopotential Height Anomaly and Vertically Integrated Moisture Flux (VIMF)

Shading shows the anomaly in 500 hPa geopotential height relative to the monthly climatology.

* Blue colours indicate anomalously low geopotential heights (troughs).
* Red colours indicate anomalously high geopotential heights (ridges).

Dashed contours show the climatological 500 hPa geopotential height field.

Vectors indicate the vertically integrated moisture flux (VIMF), illustrating the direction and magnitude of moisture transport during the event.


---

## Panel (c): Total Column Water Vapour (TCWV) and Mean Sea-Level Pressure (MSLP)

Shading shows total column water vapour (kg m(^{-2})), representing the integrated atmospheric moisture content.

Contours show mean sea-level pressure anomalies:

* Red contours indicate positive pressure anomalies.
* Blue dashed contours indicate negative pressure anomalies.

Grey contours show the absolute mean sea-level pressure field.

This panel illustrates the distribution of atmospheric moisture and associated synoptic-scale pressure systems during the event.

---

## Panel (d): ITCZ Position and 300 hPa Potential Vorticity (PV) Anomaly

Shading shows the anomaly in 300 hPa potential vorticity (PV) relative to climatology.

The black contour denotes the 2-PVU dynamical tropopause.

Two ITCZ envelopes are plotted:

* **Black band:** climatological ITCZ position.
* **Green band:** event-mean ITCZ position.

Vectors show vertically integrated moisture flux.

This panel is designed to assess the role of tropical moisture export, upper-level dynamical forcing, and possible tropical–extratropical interactions during the event.
